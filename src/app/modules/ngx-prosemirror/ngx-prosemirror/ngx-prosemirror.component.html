This step is needed for access to SSW's Azure DevOps - can take 3 days.

Linking your Microsoft account to SSW is very important. Firstly it allows us to easily allocate you MSDN licenses and secondly it allows you to link your MCP ID (if you have one) to SSW. Furthermore, it allows the SysAdmin to grant you access to Azure DevOps.

Your MSDN subscription will provide the product keys needed in the next Induction item - "Install and Configure your PC (or laptop)"

Note: It may take up to 72 hours for your SSW MSDN subscription to become available. You will not be able to sign in to Azure DevOps (ssw.visualstudio.com is the default one, some internal and client repositories are located on ssw2.visualstudio.com) until your MSDN Subscription has been activated.

To link your Microsoft Account please do the following

  1. Go to Partner Center and sign in using your SSW Account.
  2. Select "Dashboard"
  3. Select "Your Name | My profile"


  4. Select "Associate Microsoft Learning Account":


  5. When prompted for a login enter the details of your account that your MCP ID is associated with 
    • This has to be a Microsoft Account
    • This has to be registered as a Microsoft Learning Account
    • If you see yellow message below then click on the www.microsoft.com/learning link and register your account as a learning account.

      To register your account as a Learning Account, log in to the Microsoft Learning site with your Microsoft Account (personal account) credentials.
      Click on your profile avatar, and click "View Learning profile" 

      Then fill in your profile information to register your Microsoft Account as a Microsoft Learning Account

      Figure: Fill out your information on the required fields and submit


      Figure: Do *not* click the "Link" button it is for Microsoft Employees only.  Just retry associating Microsoft Learning account.

  6. Then select "Get Partner University Access"


Check that your MSDN Subscription is activated

  1. Go to Partner Center and sign in using your Microsoft Account (Personal Account)
  2. Verify that you can see SSW's Partner ID 1030936 (make sure you make the page full size otherwise this ID could be hidden)

    Figure: Step 1 - How to check you have an active MSDN Subscription

  3. Log out of your personal account and log back into the Partner Center with your SSW account. Check the two areas are now showing your information instead of the buttons:

    Figure: Step 2 - What you see when 'done' connecting the accounts

    4. Send your Done / Not Done
